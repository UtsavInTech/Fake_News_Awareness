How to Stop the Spread & Identify Fake News

Practical steps for users and community volunteers to reduce the spread of false information.

1. Pause before sharing

If a post produces a strong emotional reaction, don‚Äôt share immediately. Take a moment.

2. Check the source

Look for the original publisher. Official sites and reputable newsrooms are more reliable.

3. Cross-verify

Search the claim on multiple trusted outlets and fact-checkers before believing it.

4. Inspect media

Use reverse image search for photos and tools like InVID for video verification.

5. Educate gently

When correcting others, use facts and sources ‚Äî avoid shaming and escalate when necessary.

Quick Checklist

  1. Who posted this? Is the source known?
  2. Is supporting evidence present?
  3. Does the headline match the article content?
  4. Could this be satire or a manipulated clip?

Tools

  • Google Reverse Image Search / TinEye
  • Wayback Machine (archive.org)
  • Fact-check sites (PIB, Snopes, Boom, Factly)
